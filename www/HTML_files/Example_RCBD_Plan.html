Example of a Randomized Complete Block Design (RCBD)

Consider a study conducted to evaluate the consistency and accuracy of blood analysis results for a specific metabolite measured by three different laboratories.
The goal is to standardize procedures and ensure that the results are comparable across laboratories.

In this example, the laboratories act as a fixed factor (analogous to treatments), while the blood samples from each patient serve as blocks.
Because each patient may have unique biological characteristics that influence metabolite levels, blocking by patient accounts for this inherent subject-to-subject variability.

Each blood sample is divided into three equal aliquots, and each aliquot is randomly assigned to one of the three laboratories for analysis.
This ensures that every laboratory measures the same set of samples, allowing for a fair comparison.
