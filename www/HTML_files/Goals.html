What is the purpose of this app? HOLE

This app has been designed for simulating a Randomized Complete Block Design (RCBD) and for exploring the different aspects that determines the results. This is a SIMULATION tool and it is not a tool for analyzing real data. The app simulates experiments using a set of parameters defined by the user.

What is a simulation?

A simulation is a computational experiment used to mimic the process of conducting the experiment many times under known or assumed conditions.

It allows researchers or students to understand how random variation, block effects, and treatment differences influence the analysis of variance (ANOVA) results and conclusions — without actually performing the physical experiment.

A simulation helps you:

  • Visualize variability due to random errors and blocking.
  • Evaluate the power of detecting treatment differences under different levels of variability.
  • Explore robustness of the design when assumptions (like homogeneity of variance or normality) are violated.
  • Understand sampling distributions of test statistics (e.g., F-ratios).

Suggested workflow for using the app

You can use this app in different ways, but a suggested workflow can help:

  • Define your problem. How many treatments? How many blocks? Do blocks have an important variability? Can you obtain replicates? Which is the treatmet effect (set a value for within sigma and chose a value for Cohen’s f)
  • Introduce the parameter values in the app
  • Generate data
  • Explore the estucture of the data according to the experimental design. Try to understand the difference between RCBD and CRD.
  • Compare the analysis in both cases.
  • Compare the confidence intervals for the pairwise comparison of treatment’s effects.
  • Compute statistical power. What does it mean?
  • Explore the effect of increasing the number of blocks and replicates on statistical power.
  • Explore precision on the pairwise estimation of effects.
