Why Simulating an Scenario Helps

Simulation allows us to see the benefit of blocking in an RCBD.
In particular, it lets us compare two situations:

  1. Analyzing the data using an RCBD (blocking properly)
  2. Ignoring blocks and treating the experiment as a Completely Randomized Design (CRD)

When we simulate data with substantial block-to-block variability, we typically find:

  • RCBD maintains good power to detect treatment differences, because the block variability is separated out and does not inflate the residual noise.
  • CRD loses power, because block differences get absorbed into the residual error, making treatments look more similar than they actually are.

üìä Conclusion

Blocking reduces unexplained variability.
If blocks are ignored, the experiment becomes noisier, making true differences harder to detect.

Simulating both designs side by side provides valuable intuition for students and researchers, showing how proper blocking improves precision and statistical power.
